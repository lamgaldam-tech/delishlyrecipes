Spicy Thai Basil Stir-Fry

A fiery and aromatic stir-fry with ground pork, Thai basil, and chilies served over jasmine rice.

Prep

10 min

Cook

10 min

Servings

2

Calories

450

About Spicy Thai Basil Stir-Fry

Spicy Thai Basil Stir-Fry, known as Pad Krapow in Thailand, is a fast and aromatic dish that delivers bold flavors with minimal cooking time. Ground pork is stir-fried with fresh Thai basil leaves, garlic, and fiery chilies, resulting in a pungent and spicy dish that excites the senses. Soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce create a balanced umami base, harmonizing with the heat from chilies and the herbal aroma of basil. Served over fragrant jasmine rice, this dish is both filling and flavorful. Its quick cooking method preserves texture and freshness while ensuring the intense flavors remain vibrant. Thai Basil Stir-Fry exemplifies the balance of heat, aroma, and umami that defines authentic Thai cuisine. This dish is highly adaptable, allowing substitution of proteins such as chicken, beef, or tofu. The combination of spices, fresh herbs, and sauces produces a layered flavor profile that is simultaneously savory, spicy, and aromatic. Perfect for weeknight dinners, it requires only a few ingredients yet delivers restaurant-quality taste at home. The dish is celebrated for its simplicity, speed, and authentic flavor, making it a staple in Thai home cooking and an accessible introduction to bold Asian flavors.

How to Make

Cook jasmine rice according to package directions. Heat oil in a wok over high heat until smoking. Add minced garlic and sliced chilies, stir-frying for 30 seconds to release aroma. Add ground pork, breaking it apart, cooking 3-4 minutes until browned. Stir in soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and a pinch of sugar. Toss in Thai basil leaves, stir until wilted. Serve immediately over hot jasmine rice, optionally topping with a fried egg. Maintain high heat to achieve quick cooking and preserve aromatic flavors. Constant stirring ensures even cooking and prevents burning. Adjust chilies to desired spice level. Garnish with additional basil for freshness. The method ensures balanced texture: tender meat, slightly wilted herbs, and aromatic sauce coating each grain of rice. Variations can include vegetables like bell peppers, snap peas, or carrots to increase color and nutritional value. This recipe is ideal for quick, satisfying, and flavor-packed dinners.

Pro Tips

Use fresh Thai basil for authentic aroma. Adjust chilies for heat preference. Maintain high heat for proper stir-fry technique. Mince garlic finely for even distribution. Stir constantly to prevent burning. Serve immediately for best texture. Optional: add bell peppers or snap peas. Cook rice separately to prevent soggy texture. Use a wok if possible; pan works too. Measure sauces before cooking to streamline process. Customize spice level to taste. Garnish with extra basil leaves for aroma and presentation. Add a fried egg on top to enrich flavor and texture. This dish benefits from quick cooking, ensuring flavors are concentrated and fresh.
