Best Chicken Crust Pizza Recipe – Low Carb, Keto & High Protein

Chicken Crust Pizza is a healthy, low-carb alternative to traditional pizza that’s packed with flavor and protein. Made with a crispy chicken-based crust instead of regular dough, this recipe is perfect for those following a keto or gluten-free lifestyle while still enjoying their favorite comfort food. About Best Chicken Crust Pizza Recipe – Low Carb, Keto & High Protein

Chicken Crust Pizza is a healthy and delicious alternative to traditional pizza, using a protein-rich chicken crust instead of regular dough. This low-carb and gluten-free recipe is perfect for those following keto or high-protein diets while still enjoying a classic comfort food. The crust is made by mixing ground chicken with cheese and seasonings, then baking it until firm and slightly crispy. Once topped with sauce, cheese, and your favorite toppings, it becomes a flavorful and satisfying meal that’s both nutritious and easy to prepare.

How to Make

Step 1 Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Step 2 In a bowl, combine ground chicken, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Step 3 Spread the mixture into a thin, even crust on the prepared baking sheet. Step 4 Bake for 15–20 minutes until the crust is firm and lightly golden. Step 5 Remove from oven and add pizza sauce, cheese, and toppings. Step 6 Return to oven and bake for another 8–10 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly. Step 7 Let cool slightly, slice, and serve.

Pro Tips

✔ Spread the crust thinly for a crispier texture. ✔ Use parchment paper to prevent sticking. ✔ Pre-bake the crust fully before adding toppings. ✔ Drain excess moisture from the chicken to avoid a soggy crust. ✔ Add herbs or spices to customize flavor.

FAQ

1. Is chicken crust pizza really low carb? Yes, it replaces traditional dough, making it low in carbs and high in protein. 2. Can I make it ahead of time? Yes, you can pre-bake the crust and store it in the fridge. 3. Can I freeze chicken crust pizza? Yes, freeze the crust or the fully assembled pizza. 4. Why is my crust soggy? It may be too thick or not baked long enough before adding toppings. 5. What toppings work best? Cheese, pepperoni, mushrooms, peppers, and olives all work great.
