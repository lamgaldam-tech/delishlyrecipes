Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp

Juicy shrimp cooked in a zesty lemon garlic butter sauce, served over rice or pasta.

Prep

10 min

Cook

15 min

Servings

3

Calories

300

About Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp

Garlic Butter Shrimp is an incredibly flavorful dish that combines the rich, creamy taste of butter with the bright, zesty notes of fresh lemon, creating a balance that is both indulgent and refreshing. Each shrimp is cooked to perfection, seared just enough to develop a slightly caramelized surface while remaining tender and juicy on the inside. The minced garlic infuses the butter with aromatic intensity, creating layers of savory depth that complement the natural sweetness of the shrimp. Paprika adds a subtle smoky undertone, while salt and pepper enhance all the flavors without overpowering them. Fresh parsley sprinkled on top provides a burst of herbal brightness and a touch of color, making the dish visually appealing as well as delicious. This recipe is ideal for quick weeknight dinners, elegant date-night meals, or even as a versatile appetizer for parties. It pairs beautifully with a side of fluffy rice, buttery pasta, or even a crisp green salad. The cooking process is simple yet rewarding, requiring only a few minutes to achieve restaurant-quality results in your own kitchen. The combination of ingredients works harmoniously to produce a dish that is both comforting and impressive. The buttery sauce clings to the shrimp, ensuring every bite is rich and satisfying. Lemon juice added at the end brightens the overall flavor profile, cutting through the richness of the butter and creating a more balanced taste. The recipe is forgiving and adaptable; spices can be adjusted according to preference, and additional aromatics such as chili flakes or white wine can be incorporated to elevate the complexity. Leftovers, if any, can be gently reheated without losing texture or flavor, making it a practical choice for busy lifestyles. Garlic Butter Shrimp exemplifies how simple ingredients can combine to produce a sophisticated and deeply enjoyable dish that appeals to all ages and occasions. Its rich flavors, simple preparation, and visually appealing presentation make it a favorite in home kitchens and perfect for sharing with friends or family.

How to Make

To prepare Garlic Butter Shrimp, begin by melting butter and adding olive oil to a skillet over medium heat, allowing the mixture to warm evenly. Once the fat is heated, add the minced garlic and sauté for just a few seconds until fragrant, being careful not to let it brown or burn, as this can create bitterness. Next, add the shrimp, ensuring they are spread in a single layer in the pan, so each piece sears properly and cooks evenly. Season the shrimp with paprika, salt, and pepper, making sure every piece is coated with the spices. As the shrimp cook, they will turn a bright pink color and curl slightly, signaling that they are nearing doneness. Cooking time is short—usually just a few minutes per side—so constant attention is important to avoid overcooking, which can make the shrimp rubbery. After the shrimp are cooked through, squeeze the juice of a fresh lemon over them, allowing the citrus to meld with the garlic butter and create a vibrant, tangy flavor. Remove the skillet from the heat and sprinkle freshly chopped parsley on top to enhance aroma and presentation. Serve the shrimp immediately, either as a standalone appetizer or accompanied by rice, pasta, or vegetables. The method ensures that the shrimp remain juicy, the garlic butter sauce is smooth and flavorful, and the dish is ready to impress with minimal effort. The process is simple but emphasizes precision and timing, ensuring the perfect balance of texture and taste for every bite. This method also allows for small customizations like adding chili flakes, wine, or herbs to further enrich the flavors.

Pro Tips

When making Garlic Butter Shrimp, using fresh shrimp is essential for the best flavor and texture, as frozen shrimp can sometimes release excess water, diluting the sauce. Avoid overcrowding the skillet to allow the shrimp to sear properly and develop caramelization, which enhances taste. Mince the garlic finely and add it to the pan at the correct time to prevent bitterness while releasing its full aroma. Adjust the amount of paprika to taste, as it adds a subtle smoky warmth without overpowering the natural flavor of the shrimp. Adding lemon juice at the end balances the richness of the butter and lifts the dish, creating a more rounded flavor profile. Fresh parsley provides not only color but also a herbaceous note that complements the seafood perfectly. Serve immediately to maintain the juiciness and temperature, as reheating can change the texture slightly, though gentle reheating is possible. This dish pairs beautifully with rice, pasta, or bread to soak up the buttery sauce, making it versatile for various meals. Small variations, like incorporating white wine, chili flakes, or garlic powder, can personalize the dish according to preference. These tips ensure consistency, allowing anyone to prepare Garlic Butter Shrimp that is flavorful, tender, and visually appealing, suitable for casual dinners or special occasions alike. Attention to detail in cooking time, seasoning, and finishing touches ensures every plate is a success.

FAQ

Q: Can I use frozen shrimp? A: Yes, but thaw completely and pat dry to avoid watery sauce. Frozen shrimp often release extra liquid that can dilute the garlic butter, affecting flavor and texture. Proper thawing ensures even cooking and that the shrimp sear nicely rather than steam. Rinsing and drying them also helps the butter and seasonings cling better. Q: Can I make this dairy-free? A: Substitute butter with olive oil or vegan butter. The flavor profile will shift slightly, but using olive oil keeps the richness while allowing the garlic and spices to shine. Adjust seasoning as needed, and finish with lemon juice to brighten the dish, maintaining balance in the absence of dairy. Q: How to prevent overcooking? A: Shrimp cook very quickly, usually 2-3 minutes per side. Overcooked shrimp become rubbery and lose their sweet, tender texture. Watch for the color change to opaque pink and the curling shape. Remove immediately from heat once done. Residual heat can finish cooking slightly without overcooking. Q: Can I prep ahead? A: You can prepare the garlic butter sauce and chop ingredients in advance, but cook the shrimp just before serving. Pre-cooking the shrimp is possible, but they lose their ideal texture upon reheating. Assemble components so minimal effort is needed at the last moment to ensure freshness and juiciness. Q: Can I add extra flavors? A: Yes, customize with chili flakes for heat, white wine for depth, or fresh herbs like thyme. The recipe is versatile and forgiving, so small adjustments enhance it without ruining the base flavors. Lemon juice should be added at the end to balance richness and retain brightness. Q: What to serve with it? A: Garlic Butter Shrimp pairs well with rice, pasta, crusty bread, or a fresh salad. The buttery sauce clings to these accompaniments beautifully, turning even simple sides into a satisfying, cohesive meal. Consider serving alongside roasted vegetables for color and texture variety. Q: Can it be doubled? A: Absolutely, but cook shrimp in batches to prevent overcrowding. Overcrowding releases water, preventing proper sear and altering texture. Batch cooking ensures each shrimp cooks evenly, maintains flavor, and gets coated in garlic butter perfectly. Q: How to store leftovers? A: Store shrimp separately from sauce for up to one day in the fridge. Gently reheat to prevent toughness. Leftovers can be added to pastas or salads, but fresh cooking is always best for taste and texture. Avoid freezing if possible, as it changes the delicate texture of cooked shrimp.
